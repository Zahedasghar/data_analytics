R for political science

Data is underpinned in all areas ranging from AI, ML to socio-political domains. AI cant do things without data and same goes in all walks of life. There are two types of data: qualitative and quantitative. In qualitative data, our analysis is mainly restricted to description of facts, emotions, contents etc. Quantitative data analysis consists of exploration to causal inference. Whether one loves or loathes data, one has to understand it to have informed decision making.@ijaz2019

For this, one does not need to be a core coder or statistician but by understanding some basics data literacy one can work with most commonly tasks required with data.

Today post is use of data for political science in R. I am going to demonstrate that how one can use R in a very simple way to analyze interest of people of Pakistan regarding browsing TALIBAN using google trends.

R for journalists and political scientists

This post will help journalists and political science students to apply R tools for analyzing google trends data. There is no need to be scared from data. Data use is no more a choice but compulsion, so lets be data savy. Google trends is a well-know, free tool provided by google that helps to analyze search queries on its google search engine. Google trends is quite handy in analyzing market behavior, people’s browsing interests in films/dramas, political and economic issues browsing among many others. In this post I am going to use R to analyze some of the searches with respect to media.

Taliban

It is assumed that Pakistanis are very concerned about Taliban. To assess it, I have extracted data related to browsing on google in Pakistan related to Taliban.
